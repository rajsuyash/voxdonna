For a premium brand where the average order value exceeds 1,000 EUR, losing even a handful of customers per month to language gaps is a significant revenue problem.

The traditional solution --- hiring trilingual support agents with deep product knowledge about plancha cooking, gas connections, stainless steel care, and outdoor installation --- is neither scalable nor affordable. ## Why 60-80% of Inquiries Are the Same Questions

Before building any automation, you need to audit what your support team actually handles. Across specialty brands in the 500-5,000 EUR product range, the breakdown is remarkably consistent:

| Inquiry Type | % of Total Volume |
|---|---|
| Product specifications and pricing | 20-25% |
| Care and maintenance instructions | 15-20% |
| Warranty terms and claim process | 10-15% |
| Delivery status and shipping timelines | 10-15% |
| Product availability and stock | 8-12% |
| Installation and setup guidance | 5-10% |
| Complex troubleshooting or complaints | 10-15% |

The first six categories are repetitive, factual, and perfectly suited for automation. They account for 68-82% of all inquiries depending on the brand. Only the last category --- complex troubleshooting and genuine complaints --- requires a human with judgment and empathy.

For a brand like Le Marquier, the most common question in each language is some variation of: *"What is the difference between the Plancha Prestige 600 and the Plancha Prestige 750?"* followed closely by *"How do I season and maintain my plancha surface?"* These questions have definitive answers that do not change. Having a skilled support agent answer them fifty times a month is a waste of talent.

The goal is not to replace your support team. It is to free them from repetitive work so they can focus on the 20% of inquiries that actually require human expertise --- the complex warranty disputes, the custom installation consultations, and the high-value upsell conversations.

## Step-by-Step: Building an AI FAQ System Trained on Your Product Knowledge

### What You Need Before Starting

1. **Product catalog** in PDF or structured format (specs, pricing, descriptions)
2. **Care and maintenance guides** for each product line
3. **Warranty documentation** (terms, conditions, claim process)
4. **Top 50 most frequently asked questions** from your support inbox (export from your helpdesk or compile manually)
5. **Brand voice guidelines** --- even informal notes on tone help significantly

### The Setup Process

**Step 1: Structure Your Knowledge Base**

Organize your product information into clear categories. For Le Marquier, this might look like:

- Product lines (Plancha Prestige, Plancha Tradition, Outdoor Kitchens, Accessories)
- Per-product specs (dimensions, materials, gas type, BTU output, weight)
- Care guides (seasoning, daily cleaning, deep cleaning, winter storage)
- Warranty (duration, what is covered, what voids the warranty, claim process)

**Step 2: Upload and Train**

Modern AI platforms accept PDFs, Word documents, website URLs, and structured data. Upload everything. The AI parses your documentation and builds an internal knowledge graph that it references when answering questions.

Critical: review the AI's initial responses against your top 50 FAQs. Correct any inaccuracies. This training loop typically takes 2-4 hours of focused work and dramatically improves answer quality.

**Step 3: Configure Multilingual Responses**

The AI does not need separate knowledge bases for each language. A single knowledge base in French (your primary documentation language) is sufficient. Modern large language models translate context accurately while preserving technical terminology. When a customer asks in English about *"cleaning a stainless steel plancha,"* the AI draws from your French care guide and responds naturally in English.

Set language detection to automatic. The system identifies the customer's language from their first message and responds accordingly.

**Step 4: Deploy Across Channels**

Embed the FAQ chatbot on your website (all language versions), connect it to your Facebook Messenger, and integrate with your email system for auto-suggested replies. A single deployment covers all three languages simultaneously.

**Expected result:** 60-70% of written inquiries resolved without human intervention within the first 30 days.

## Step-by-Step: Multilingual Voice Support

Written support handles email and chat, but premium brands still receive a significant volume of phone calls. Customers spending 2,000 EUR on an outdoor kitchen want to talk to someone.

### How AI Voice Agents Work

Unlike legacy IVR systems that force callers through numbered menus (*"Press 1 for French, press 2 for English..."*), modern AI voice agents operate conversationally. The caller simply speaks, and the system:

1. **Detects the language** within the first 2-3 seconds of speech
2. **Responds natively** in that language --- not translated speech, but natural, fluent conversation
3. **Accesses the same knowledge base** as your written FAQ system
4. **Escalates to a human** when the inquiry falls outside its confidence threshold

### Setup Walkthrough

**Step 1: Choose Your Voice Profiles**

Select voice characteristics for each language. For a premium French brand, you might choose a warm, professional female voice for French, a clear British English voice for the UK market, and a standard Italian voice for Italy. Consistency with brand identity matters.

**Step 2: Define Conversation Flows**

Map out the primary call reasons and the information the AI needs to collect:

- **Product inquiry:** Which product line? What specific question?
- **Order status:** Order number or customer email for lookup
- **Warranty claim:** Product, purchase date, description of issue
- **General support:** Route based on detected topic

**Step 3: Set Escalation Rules**

Define when the AI hands off to a human: after two failed attempts to resolve, when the caller explicitly requests a person, or when the topic involves a complaint or safety concern. During off-hours, the AI takes a detailed message and schedules a callback.

**Step 4: Test With Native Speakers**

Before going live, have native speakers in all three languages call the system with realistic scenarios. Test edge cases: heavy accents, background noise, code-switching between languages mid-call.

**Expected result:** 50-60% of calls fully resolved by the AI voice agent. Average call handling time reduced by 40%.

## Step-by-Step: Automating Warranty Claim Intake

Warranty claims are one of the most time-consuming support processes for premium brands. A typical manual claim for a product like a Le Marquier plancha involves:

- 10-15 minutes on the phone collecting details
- Back-and-forth emails requesting photos of the defect
- Manual entry into a tracking spreadsheet or CRM
- Internal routing to the technical team for assessment
- Follow-up communication with the customer

Total elapsed time per claim: 30-45 minutes of staff effort spread over 3-5 days.

### The Automated Process

**Step 1: Smart Intake Form**

Deploy a guided warranty claim form (accessible via chatbot, website, or voice agent) that collects:

- Product name and model (dropdown from your catalog)
- Purchase date and proof of purchase (photo upload)
- Description of the defect (structured fields plus free text)
- Photos of the issue (up to 5 images, with guidance on what to photograph)
- Customer contact details and preferred language

**Step 2: Auto-Validation**

The AI validates the claim before submission: Is the product still within warranty? Is the reported issue covered under warranty terms? If the product is out of warranty, the AI explains this immediately and offers alternative options (paid repair, replacement parts).

**Step 3: Severity Routing**

Claims are automatically categorized:

- **Low severity** (cosmetic issues, minor surface scratches): Auto-approved for replacement part shipment
- **Medium severity** (functional defect, component failure): Routed to technical team with full documentation attached
- **High severity** (safety concern, gas leak report): Flagged for immediate human review with priority notification

**Step 4: Status Updates**

The customer receives automated updates in their language at each stage: claim received, under review, approved/denied, resolution shipped.

**Expected result:** Claim intake time reduced from 45 minutes to 5 minutes. Customer satisfaction on warranty interactions increases by 25-30% due to faster resolution and proactive communication.

## Step-by-Step: Order Status Automation Across Channels

Order status inquiries are the single easiest category to automate, yet many specialty brands still handle them manually. A customer who just spent 1,800 EUR on a Plancha Prestige wants to know where it is, and they want to know now --- not after waiting on hold for 12 minutes.

### Unified Status Tracking

**Step 1: Connect Your Order Management System**

Integrate your e-commerce platform (Shopify, WooCommerce, PrestaShop, or custom) with your AI support system via API. This gives the AI real-time access to order status, tracking numbers, and estimated delivery dates.

**Step 2: Enable Multi-Channel Access**

Customers should be able to check their order status through whichever channel they prefer:

- **Website chatbot:** Customer provides order number or email, gets instant status
- **WhatsApp:** Customer sends a message, AI responds with current status and tracking link
- **Phone:** Voice agent looks up the order and provides verbal status update
- **Email:** Auto-reply with status details when the AI detects an order status inquiry

**Step 3: Proactive Notifications**

Go beyond reactive support. Send automated updates when:

- Order is confirmed and processing
- Order has shipped (with tracking number and estimated delivery)
- Delivery is scheduled for tomorrow
- Delivery has been completed (with a follow-up satisfaction check)

All notifications go out in the customer's preferred language, detected from their original purchase or support interaction.

**Step 4: Exception Handling**

For delayed or problematic orders, the AI escalates to a human but provides the customer with immediate acknowledgment: *"I can see your order has been delayed. I have flagged this for our team, and someone will contact you within 4 hours with an update."* This is vastly better than silence.

**Expected result:** Order status inquiries drop to near-zero human involvement. Customer satisfaction scores for delivery experience increase by 20%.
